The future of generative artificial intelligence in healthcare: Navigating the paradigm shift

Sarah J. Connor a, b, * Marcus Wright a Miles Dyson c

Abstract

Generative artificial intelligence (AI), particularly large language models (LLMs), has recently demonstrated remarkable capabilities across various domains. In healthcare, these technologies offer unprecedented opportunities to revolutionize diagnostic workflows, patient communication, and medical research. This paper explores the transformative potential of generative AI, examining both near-term applications and long-term implications for the healthcare paradigm. 1. Introduction

The intersection of computing and medicine has historically driven significant advancements in patient care. However, the advent of generative AI represents a foundational shift from computational tools that merely organize or analyze structured data, to systems capable of synthesizing novel unstructured information [1].

Unlike previous predictive AI models trained for specific, narrow tasks (e.g., classifying medical images), generative models—particularly those based on the Transformer architecture [2]—exhibit zero-shot capabilities that allow them to perform competently across a wide array of unforeseen healthcare scenarios.

4. Conclusion

Generative AI will undoubtedly serve as a critical catalyst in the next era of medical innovation. While challenges regarding data privacy, algorithmic bias, and clinical validation remain substantial, the trajectory points toward a synergistic future where human clinical expertise is augmented by the synthesizing power of large foundational models.
